Our Story

More than six years of open development, condensed into a few milestones.

RocketPy started as an internal tool at Projeto Jupiter, the student rocket design team at the University of São Paulo (USP), created by Giovani Ceotto to simulate the flight of the team's rockets with high fidelity. What began as a way to answer “how high will it fly?” became a full six-degrees-of-freedom trajectory simulator.

In 2021, the project was formalized in a peer-reviewed paper in the Journal of Aerospace Engineering, and RocketPy stepped out of a single team and into the global rocketry community. Its results have been validated against real flight data from teams such as the EPFL Rocket Team and the Notre Dame Rocket Team.

Since then, dozens of students, engineers and open-source contributors from around the world have shaped RocketPy — through the milestone 1.0 release in 2023 and a steady cadence of versions ever since. Everyone listed below is part of that history, marked here for good.

  1. 2018

    Moved to GitHub

    Giovani Ceotto brings the simulator into version control at Projeto Jupiter — the first public commit.

  2. 2020

    First public release

    RocketPy is packaged and released to the community (v0.9.5), open for anyone to install and use.

  3. 2021

    Published research

    “RocketPy: Six Degree-of-Freedom Rocket Trajectory Simulator” is published in the Journal of Aerospace Engineering.

  4. 2023

    Version 1.0

    A matured, stable API and a fast-growing worldwide community of contributors.

  5. Today

    Still climbing

    Actively developed and maintained, powering student teams, researchers and companies across the globe.
